Robotic Ashtray

Robotics • Python • OpenCV • Raspberry Pi • Linux

Genesis

This project was inspired by the butter-passing robot from Rick and Morty. In the show, a small robot asks its creator, “What is my purpose?”, and upon hearing “You pass butter,” promptly has an existential crisis. In a similar spirit, I’ve always found the idea of overengineering simple problems both amusing and oddly satisfying. While I deeply appreciate clean and elegant engineering solutions, there’s something entertaining, almost artistic, about deliberately building something needlessly complex that still works beautifully. So, I decided to build a robotic ashtray. When a person drops a small rectangular object butt onto its tray, the robot picks it up and places it neatly into a smaller ashtray. Hardly a world-changing invention, but it was a fun and effective sandbox for experimenting with robotic design and control.

Technical Overview

Mechanical Design

  • Designed from scratch; joints modelled in Fusion 360 and 3D-printed (PLA).
  • Four-servo arm in a classic layout:
    • Base rotation (1× servo)
    • Radial positioning (2× servos)
    • Gripper actuation (1× servo)
  • Kinematic characterization to support motion planning and control.

Perception & Control

  • Runs on a Raspberry Pi 3 (Linux) controlling servos and an RGB camera.
  • Python with OpenCV:
    • Grayscale conversion → edge detection → rectangular contour search,
    • heuristics to confirm cigarette-butt candidates.
  • Inverse kinematics converts detections to servo commands for pick-and-place.
