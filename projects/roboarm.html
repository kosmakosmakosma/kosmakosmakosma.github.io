Robotic Ashtray

Personal project • Robotics • Python • OpenCV • Raspberry Pi

Gallery

Genesis

Inspired by the butter-passing robot from Rick and Morty, I explored the joy of intentionally overengineering a simple task. The result: a robotic ashtray that picks up a cigarette butt placed on its tray and deposits it neatly into a smaller ashtray. It’s a lighthearted idea, but a great sandbox for real robotics design and control.

Technical Overview

Mechanical Design

  • Designed from scratch; joints modelled in Fusion 360 and 3D-printed (PLA).
  • Four-servo arm in a classic layout:
    • Base rotation (1× servo)
    • Radial positioning (2× servos)
    • Gripper actuation (1× servo)
  • Kinematic characterization to support motion planning and control.

Perception & Control

  • Runs on a Raspberry Pi 3 (Linux) controlling servos and an RGB camera.
  • Python stack with OpenCV:
    • Grayscale conversion → edge detection → rectangular contour search,
    • heuristics to confirm cigarette-butt candidates.
  • Inverse kinematics converts detections to servo commands for pick-and-place.
