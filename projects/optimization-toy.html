Optimization Toy

2025 • Tools: HTML, CSS, MathJax

We solve a simple constrained optimization problem:

$$ \min_{x \in \mathbb{R}^2} \; f(x) = x_1^2 + 2x_2^2 \quad \text{s.t. } \; g(x) = x_1 + x_2 - 1 = 0. $$

The Lagrangian is $$ \mathcal{L}(x, \lambda) = x_1^2 + 2x_2^2 + \lambda(x_1 + x_2 - 1). $$ Setting gradients to zero: $$ \nabla_x \mathcal{L} = \begin{bmatrix} 2x_1 + \lambda \\ 4x_2 + \lambda \end{bmatrix} = 0, \quad \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial \lambda} = x_1 + x_2 - 1 = 0. $$

From the first two equations, $x_1 = -\lambda/2$ and $x_2 = -\lambda/4$. Enforce the constraint $x_1 + x_2 = 1 \Rightarrow -\lambda/2 - \lambda/4 = 1 \Rightarrow \lambda = -\tfrac{4}{3}$. Hence $x_1^\star = \tfrac{2}{3}$ and $x_2^\star = \tfrac{1}{3}$.
